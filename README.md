After *countless* unfinished side projects (we don't talk about the graveyard 🪦), I decided to do something radical:

**Finish one.** ✅

So I'm putting my full attention into **Intensity** — a small, closed product for couples and friends who want deeper connection through shared experiences. Collect unusual ideas in themed boxes, rate how intense they feel, then draw one when you're together and actually live it. 🎲

Why something this simple?

Because personal build time is short. I wanted a scope that was *realistic* — not another ambitious architecture demo that dies in week three. I chose to invest in **creativity** and **quality**, the parts of myself I actually like showing off, more than in proving how clever the backend diagram looks. 🎨

Architecture is important. I care about it a lot. But quality in architecture is hard to *see*. Quality in a product you can open, touch, and feel? That's visible. Intensity is my way of saying: *this is what I mean by well-crafted* — in a small, finished thing.

And yes — that same bar is what I bring to the messier, more complex systems work too. The craft just shows up differently there.
